Date

Event

1881

 April 3 Begins designing rotary electric meter.
April 7 Contracts for Armington and Sims steam engine for large dynamo
April 14 Begins designing disk dynamo
April 20 Assigns Edward Acheson to learn details of lamp manufacture before going to Paris
April
  1. Enters into informal partnership with Sigmund Bergmann and Edward Johnson for manufacture of lamp sockets and accessories.
  2. Begins making speciﬁc arrangements for exhibit at Exposition Internationale de l’Électricité in Paris.
  3. Directs Alfred Haid to conduct experiments on coating ﬁlaments with various forms of carbon.
  4. Directs Charles Hughes to resume experiments on preservation of perishable foods in a vacuum
May 9 Purchases buildings in Harrison, New Jersey, for enlarged lamp factory
May 12 Charles Batchelor sails from England to New York to begin work on exhibits for the Paris Exposition
May Constructs magnetic ore separator for recovering iron ore.
June
  1. Travels to Quogue, Long Island, to inspect operation of ore separator.
  2. Begins testing large dynamo for Paris Exposition.
  3. Samuel Insull begins dictating some outgoing correspondence to a typist.
July 15
  1. Discharges most remaining employees at Menlo Park laboratory and machine shop.
  2. Completes reconstruction of large dynamo armature.
July 18 Receives nearly $55,300 in partial liquidation of his telephone interests in Great Britain
July 19 With Patrick Kenny, prepares British patent speciﬁcation for improvements in facsimile telegraphs.
July 21 Sends Charles Batchelor to Paris to oversee exhibit at Paris Exposition
July
  1. Begins designing standard dynamo models for isolated lighting plants.
  2. Devises alternative armature winding patterns to reduce internal arcing.
August 8 Conducts experiments on reducing resistance between dynamo commutator bars and brushes
August 11 Representatives in Paris begin legal action against display of Hiram Maxim’s incandescent lamps at International Electrical Exposition.
August 23 Approves terms for consolidating control of his telephone patents in Europe with those of other inventors
August 25 Edison lighting exhibit at International Electrical Exposition commences
August 28 Travels to Michigan to meet Mary Edison.
August
  1. Encourages his representatives to cultivate relationships with respected technical writers in France and Great Britain.
  2. Begins construction at 255–257 Pearl Street for New York central station plant.
  3. Begins renovation of factory in Harrison, New Jersey.
  4. Directs Charles Hughes to conduct preliminary survey for new electric railroad line at Menlo Park
September 7 Large dynamo shipped to Paris.
c.September 7 Returns from trip to Michigan.
September 14 Agrees with Henry Villard to build new experimental electric railroad
September 21 Sends Edward Johnson to London to oversee commercial development of lighting system in Great Britain
September 22 Begins laying underground conductors for New York central station district
September 23 Proposes terms for ﬁnancing construction of European factories for electric lighting equipment
September Edison Ore Milling Co. begins using iron ore separator at Quonocontaug, Rhode Island.
October 2 Instructs Edward Johnson to begin disclaimer process to strengthen British electric lighting patents
October 4 Successfully tests large dynamo for London demonstration central station at Holborn Viaduct
October 18 Foyer of Paris Opera illuminated by Edison installation
October 21 Direct-connected “C” dynamo begins operation at International Exposition
October 25 Gives notice of intention to end ﬁnancial support for Science
November 6 Agrees in principle to terms for establishing Edison electric light and manufacturing companies in Europe.
November 12 Begins period of intensive experimentation at lamp factory.
December 1 Recalls John Branner from South American vegetable ﬁber search.
December 20 Mary Edison hosts party at Menlo Park.
Fall Establishes Testing Room at the Edison Machine Works.
December 27 Receives 25 U.S. patents
December 28 Vacates New York City apartment, returning to Menlo Park home.
December
  1. Devises audible voltage indicator for isolated lighting installations.
  2. Appointed ofﬁcer of the French National Order of the Legion of Honor.

1882

 January 17 Edward Johnson unofﬁcially inaugurates Edison’s exhibition at Crystal Palace
January Construction of underground conductor network in New York halted by winter weather.
February 1
  1. Hires patent attorney Richard Dyer
  2. Fundamental British telephone patent upheld in court.
February 2 Compagnie Continentale Edison (holding company) and companies for manufacturing and isolated lighting in France organized
February 14 Sends Francis Jehl to London with standard electric meter. Ships second “C” dynamo to Holborn Viaduct in London
February 20 Arrives in Washington, D.C.
February 22
  1. Returns from Washington.
  2. Mary Edison arrives in Aiken, South Carolina.
February 25 Electrical Exhibition opens at Crystal Palace in London.
March 1 Travels to Florida to join family on vacation
March 15 Edison Electric Light Co., Ltd., organized in London
March 28 Returns to Menlo Park from Florida
March Negotiates royalty payment on dynamos to Siemens Brothers, London
Winter Writes detailed suggestions for revisions of British electric light and power patents
April 1 Production stops at Edison Lamp Co. factory in Menlo Park.
April 9 Orders rail car from J. G. Brill & Co. for electric railway experiments
April 13 Central station demonstration plant on Holborn Viaduct formally opens
April 21 Receives settlement of British telephone accounts.
April
  • Incapacitated by illness for several weeks.
  • Experimental electric freight locomotive completed.
May 8 Moves back to Menlo Park.
May 12 Resumes designing voltage-reduction devices for high voltage distribution system.
May 29 Declares opposition to proposed changes in U.S. patent law
May 31 Edison Lamp Co. resumes production in Harrison, N.J.
May
  • Purchases Menlo Park factory building from Lamp Co.
  • Directs tests of new K dynamo.
  • Agrees in principle to terms establishing Edison’s Indian & Colonial Electric Light Co. for British colonies.
June 2 Drafts patent application for cellulose lamp ﬁlament.
June 15 Approves ﬁling disclaimers of key British electric lighting patents
June 21 Edward Johnson returns from England.
Spring Designs high voltage direct current distribution system
July 1-6 Borrows over $37,000 from Drexel, Morgan & Co
July 8 Leaves on sailing trip
July 14 Reaches Montreal
July 18 Returns to Menlo Park
July
  • Experiments with chemical aids to lamp exhaustion.
  • Designs three-wire electrical distribution system.
  • Investigates malfeasance of patent attorney Zenas Wilber.
  • Construction of New York central station largely completed
August 8 Enlarges responsibilities of patent attorney Richard Dyer
August 22 Receives 20 U.S. patents
August Selects Roselle, New Jersey, for demonstration “Village Plant” electrical system
September 2 Executes formal partnership agreement with Sigmund Bergmann and Edward Johnson
September 4 Begins commercial operation of New York central station
September 19 Receives 31 U.S. patents
Summer Charles Batchelor opens Edison factories near Paris
September 26 Successfully operates two Jumbo dynamos in tandem for ﬁrst time
October 1 Leases house at 25 Gramercy Park South, New York City, for family.
October Establishes laboratory on top ﬂoor of Bergmann & Co. building in New York
November 10-11 Visits Boston.
November 15 Attends National Academy of Sciences dinner in New York.
November 16 Opens Pearl Street station to National Academy of Sciences.
December 13 Visits Boston
December 18 Loses lawsuit by Lucy Seyfert; $5,065 judgment entered.
December Suspends experiments on storage batteries

1883

 January 19 First village plant opens at Roselle, New Jersey.
February 1Sends Edward Johnson to London for consultation with Edison Electric Light Co., Ltd.
February 15Travels to Boston to begin establishment of an illuminating company
February 19Begins a trip to the South.
February 27Returns from the South ahead of schedule.
March 29Agrees to form Edison Construction Department with Samuel Insull and Edward Johnson.
