1883

 April 4-5 Travels to Albany and electrical exhibition in Cornwall,Ontario
April 16 Conducts Porﬁrio Díaz and his wife on a tour of Edison lighting plants and manufacturing shops in New York City
April 20 Agrees in principle to combine electric railroad patents with those of Stephen Field in a new company
May 3 Gives Samuel Insull power of attorney to sign contracts and conduct all business related to his Central Station Construction Department
May 3-5 Travels to Shamokin, Pennsylvania, and signs contract to construct ﬁrst village plant central station in Sunbury, Pennsylvania
May 15-16 Requests several agents for Edison electric light to provide information regarding the use of the Maxim light in their region
May 18 Appoints William Rich as superintendent of construction for central station plants
June 1 Discharges all domestic servants at family home in New York City
June 1 Hires Frank Sprague
June 18-19 ravels to Chicago for the Electric Railway Exhibition
Spring Designs short-core dynamo
June 28 Comitato per Applicazioni Dell Elettricità Edison inaugu- rates central station service in Milan, Italy
June Spends substantial but indeterminate period supervising construction of ﬁrst three-wire village plant in Sunbury, Pennsylvania
July 3-12 Supervises completion and 4 July start of Sunbury station
July 17 Places Charles Campbell in charge of Engineering Depart- ment making estimates for central stations
July 23 Donates to telegraphers’ strike relief fund
July 24 Gives conditional approval to merger of his British electric light interests with those of Joseph Swan
August 1 New York Evening Post publishes interview in which Edison pledges to become “simply a business man for a year.”
August 4-13 Mary Edison, daughter Marion, and sister Alice vacation in Long Beach, Long Island, with Edison and Alice’s husband, William Holzer, joining them for two days
August 18 Fires superintendent Charles Dean and bookkeeper Charles Rocap from Edison Machine Works
September 18-26 Supervises completion and 20 September start of Shamokin, Pennsylvania, central station
Summer Adopts Frank Sprague’s mathematical method of determining conductors for central stations
September 29 Travels to Fall River and Brockton, Massachusetts
October 1 Inaugurates Brockton central station
October 1 Edison family moves to Clarendon Hotel, New York.
October 2 Final board approval for organization of Edison and Swan United Electric Light Co., Ltd., retroactive to 30 June 1883
October 6 Returns to New York
October 8 U.S. Commissioner of Patents rules in favor of Sawyer-Man patent on carbon loop ﬁlament
October 13 Devises new voltage regulator based on “Edison Effect” lamp
November 13 Retains attorney John Tomlinson for lamp patent infringe- ment lawsuits
November 14 Testiﬁes as plaintiff ’s witness in lawsuit against overhead electric lines in New York City
December 11
  1. Repays more than $35,000 borrowed from Drexel, Morgan & Co
  2. Sends Samuel Insull to Great Britain and continental Europe
December 17-18 Travels to Fall River, Massachusetts, to inaugurate central station there
December 24 Begins experimenting with gelatinous materials for lamp ﬁlaments
December Makes preliminary efforts to license and sell duplicating ink

1884

 January 5 Visits Henry Villard to console him after his ﬁnancial collapse
January 24 German patent ofﬁce rules all carbon-ﬁlament incandescent lamps subject to Edison’s patent
January 27 Samuel Insull returns
January 29 Anthony Thomas proposes to Edison and other Edison Electric directors that all the Edison lighting companies and shops be reorganized into a single company
Janauary Directs research on electrodeposition of metallic ﬁlms. Incorporates Edison Lamp Co. and Edison Machine Works
February 1
  1. Appoints Willis Stewart as his electric lighting agent in Chile.
  2. Charles Clarke ofﬁcially resigns as chief engineer of Edison Electric Light Co.
February 5 Drafts contract for central station engineers
February 10 Leaves on Florida vacation with Mary Edison
February 23 Swedish patent ofﬁce voids several Edison patents for lack of use in the country
February
  1. Withdraws “Edison Effect” voltage indicator.
  2. Loses retrial of civil suit brought by Lucy Seyfert
February-March Fills pocket notebook with plans for experiments while on Florida vacation
March 24 Is sent copies of the “Dot and Dash” Polka, named after Edison’s children, Marion and Thomas, Jr., by composer and former telegrapher John Milliken.
March 31 Returns to New York with Mary Edison
April 9 Father-in-law Nicholas Stilwell dies at Menlo Park.
April 22-26 Edison unwell and cancels planned trip to Newburgh, New York
April 24 Announces intention to disband Construction Department
April 28 Drafts letter with Edward Johnson, Samuel Insull, and Francis Upton to Henry Villard regarding Edison Electic Light Co.’s Committee on Manufacturing and Reorganization
April-May Works to pass bill in New Jersey legislature authorizing electric lighting companies to put up poles or lay wires under the streets
May 1
  1. Moves family back to Gramercy Park house
  2. Frank Sprague resigns
May 1-2 Demonstrates electric lighting system at public exhibition in Worcester, Massachusetts
May 3 Charles Batchelor sails back to New York.
May 7 Accepts nomination as one of the vice presidents of the newly formed American Institute of Electrical Engineers
May 15 Prepares caveat and has John Ott conduct experiments to use an electromagnet to separate nonferrous ores.
May 21 Appoints Charles Batchelor general superintendent of Edi- son Machine Works
May 22 Has John Ott conduct experiments on using dynamos for telegraphy in preparation for promoting their use for that purpose
May 24 Asks Edison Electric Light Co. for authority over South American lighting business and subsequently receives it
May 28 Negotiates retainer with Richard Dyer and Henry Seely to serve as his patent attorneys.
June 1 Article about Mary Edison published in New York World
June 18 Charles Coster and Grosvenor Lowrey propose reorgan- ization of Edison lighting companies.
June-early July Organizes Edison Shafting Co.
  1. Gives up ofﬁce space at 65 Fifth Avenue.
  2. Begins week of sustained experimentation on direct conversion of coal into electricity.
  3. Sheriff ’s sale of Menlo Park property held to satisfy judgment in Seyfert lawsuit.
August 9 Mary Edison dies at Menlo Park.
August 23 Accepts appointment as a member of the “National Conference of Electricians
September 1
  1. Signs stockholding agreements with manufacturing shops as part of transfer of the Edison Construction Department’s business to Edison Co. for Isolated Lighting.
  2. Signs lease for top ﬂoor of residence at 39 E. 18th Street, New York.
September 4-6 In Philadelphia with Marion to attend International Electri- cal Exhibition
September 15 Gives up lease on Gramercy Park home
September 16-19 Attends International Electrical Exhibition in Philadelphia with daughter Marion
September 24 Returns to Philadelphia for several days
October 8 Asks Francis Upton to have lamp factory prepare special stage effect lamps for Koster & Bial’s Concert Hall in New York.
October 16-25 Seeks proxies from Edison Electric stockholders.
October 21 Second sheriff ’s sale of Edison property at Menlo Park held to satisfy judgment in Seyfert lawsuit
October 28 Edison Electric stockholders elect combination ticket to board of directors
November 10 Attends Sullivan-Laﬂin ﬁght at Madison Square Garden
November 15 Proposes to William Brewster to establish a syndicate for the electric light business outside of Europe and the United States
November 28 Attends meeting of the Edison Electric Light Co. of Eu- rope at his laboratory to discuss reorganization of French companies
Fall Experiments on telephone technology for and negotiates contract with American Bell Telephone Co
December 20-24 Visits Boston in connection with telephone experiments and contract negotiation with American Bell.
December 21 Attends Boston Electrical Exhibition.
December Designs special theater lighting for use at Steele MacKaye’s Lyceum Theater and the Bijou Theatre in Boston.
