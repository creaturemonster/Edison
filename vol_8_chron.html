Year Date Event

1885

 January 2-6 Makes brief trip to Boston, likely to continue negotiations regarding his work on American Bell Co.’s long distance lines and the Western Union’s rights to his telephone research; probably revisits the Electrical Exhibition.
Janaury 23-26 Visits Boston
February 20 Leaves New York with Ezra Gilliland on extended trip
February 23 Agrees with Ezra Gilliland to transfer joint patent rights for railway telegraphy to the Railway Telegraph and Tele- phone Co., a company incorporated in New York during this month.
February 28 Visits the World Industrial and Cotton Centennial Exposi- tion in New Orleans
March 5-21 Vacations in Florida with his daughter and Ezra and Lillian Gilliland; contracts to buy land in Fort Myers
Winter-Spring Conducts railway telegraph experiments
June 3 Dispatches Francis Upton to Europe as his representative for electric light business.
June 2-9 Visits Ezra Gilliland in Boston vicinity
June 27-July 8 Visit to Gilliland overlaps with visit of Mina Miller
July 14 Arrives for extended stay at Gilliland’s vacation cottage at Winthrop, Mass
July 26-August 5 Sails with Gilliland and others
August 10-18 Visits the Miller family at the Chautauqua Institution
August 18-31 Travels with Marion, Mina, and others to Niagara Falls, Montreal, and New Hampshire; proposes marriage to Mina.
September 4 Authorizes settlement of major outstanding debts due him for construction of central station generating plants
September 19 Dispatches Alfred Tate to set up commercial trial of phono- plex system in Canada.
September 21-30 Visits the Gillilands in Boston
September 28 Edison household moves to Normandie Hotel in New York
September 30 Asks Mina Miller’s father for permission to marry.
November 8 With others, incorporates International Railway Telegraph and Telephone Co
October-November Designs improved phonoplex telegraph receiver. Executes ﬁve patent applications covering phonoplex and multiple telegraph inventions.
November Joined in New York by Ezra Gilliland as a business and inventive partner
December 8-10 Designs apparatus for experiments to discover an unknown force he calls XYZ
December 13 Leaves to visit the Miller family in Akron, Ohio

1886

 January 20 Purchases the former country home of Henry Pedder in Llewellyn Park, N.J
January 30 Merges Electric Tube Co. and the Edison Shafting Co. into Edison Machine Works
January Agrees to pay judgment and court costs in long-running Seyfert lawsuit
February 1 Participates in public demonstration of railway telegraph system on Staten Island
February 16 Incorporates Sims-Edison Electric Torpedo Co
February 24 Marries Mina Miller in Akron, Ohio
February 27 Arrives in Florida on honeymoon
March 17 Arrives in Fort Myers
March 18 Begins proliﬁc series of notebook entries witnessed by Mina.
March-April Plans extensive planting and landscaping of Fort Myers estate
April 29 Arrives in Akron with Mina
May 4 Takes up residence at Glenmont
May 17 Strike at Edison Machine Works in New York City.
June 11 Begins lamp experiments at Edison Lamp Co. factory in East Newark and moves his laboratory there.
June 23 Announces decision to move Edison Machine Works to Schenectady, N.Y.
July 2 Incorporates the Edison United Manufacturing Co., which takes over sales and installation work of the Edison Co. for Isolated Lighting.
August 11-12 Attends meeting of Association of Edison Illuminating Cos. at Long Beach, Long Island
October 5 Commences work on new “standard” phonograph.
October 25 Drafts ﬁrst of several patent applications for transmission and distribution of high-voltage currents
October
  1. Reaches agreement in principle with Charles Porter for the development, manufacture, and sale of advanced steam engines.
  2. Drafts an informal critique of alternating current on engi- neering, economic, and public safety grounds
November 10 Drafts comprehensive memorandum to Edward Johnson against high-voltage alternating current.
November 26 Edison Electric Light Co. licenses Zipernowsky alternating current transformer patents in United States
December 18 Edison Machine Works moves to Schenectady.
December 23 Edison Electric Light Co. initiates infringement suit against Westinghouse Electric and others, including its representa- tives (the “Trenton Feeder Case”).
December 30 Becomes very ill with pleurisy.
December 31 Edison Company for Isolated Lighting is absorbed by the Edison Electric Light Company
November to December Completes at least ten patent applications related to high- voltage electric transmission and distribution.

1887

 January Conﬁned to Llewellyn Park home with pleurisy
February 8
  1. Decides to sell $80,000 of Edison Illuminating stock and loan the money to the Edison Machine Works.
  2. Resigns as president and director of Edison Electric Light Co. of Europe, Ltd.
February 9 Departs for Florida in care of a nurse
February 16 Appointed to committee on uniform installation standards at convention of National Electric Light Association (Philadelphia).
February 21 Edison Electric Light Co. absorbs Western Edison Co.
March 1 Drafts ﬁrst patent application and begins experiments on pyromagnetic motor.
March 8-21 Visited at Fort Myers by Charles Batchelor, who assists with pyromagnetic motor experiments
March 24 Treated surgically for facial abcess
March Alfred Tate negotiates license of Edison’s stencil patents allowing A. B. Dick Co. to market mimeograph machine.
April 6 Instructs Batchelor to begin planning new laboratory at Orange, N.J
April 8 Suffers recurrence of facial abcess.
April 30 Returns from the South in improved health.
May 1-3 Drafts notes for lamp experiments for laboratory staff.
May 3 Hires Henry Hudson Holly as the architect for new laboratory
May 7 Begins sustained experiments on phonographs and recording cylinders.
May 18 Drafts ﬁrst patent application for pyromagnetic generator
May 19
  1. Approves architectural plans for new laboratory.
  2. Goes to Schenectady.
May 21 Drafts patent application for improved phonograph cylinder and electrostatic playback mechanism
June 5 Has William K. L. Dickson begin sustained magnetic ore separator experiments
June 8 Drafts patent application for improved municipal lamp “cutout.”
July 5 Shops for laboratory equipment in Philadelphia; probably travels to Baltimore and Washington.
July 30 Dismisses architect Henry Hudson Holly and replaces him with Joseph Taft.
July
  1. Begins planning staff of new laboratory.
  2. Decides to add four small laboratory buildings
August 15 Has papers on pyromagnetic dynamo and magnetic bridge presented at meeting of the American Association for the Advancement of Science
August
  1. Solicits investment in prospective company for manufactur- ing inventions created at new laboratory.
  2. Devises ﬁve-wire electrical distribution system.
September Designates George Gouraud his agent for the phonograph in Great Britain.
October 1 Contracts for the manufacture of phonographic dolls and toys
October 8 Incorporates the Edison Phonograph Co.
October 25 Drafts patent application on battery device to convert alter- nating to direct current for motors.
October 27 Holds board meeting at Glenmont to reorganize the Edison United Manufacturing Co.
October 28 Assigns all U.S. rights for the phonograph to the Edison Phonograph Co., which appoints Ezra Gilliland its sole general agent.
November 11 Hosts visitors at laboratory from National Academy of Sciences meeting.
October-mid-November Gives newspaper interviews about the forthcoming phono- graph and new laboratory at Orange.
November 15-25 Makes extensive notes on experiments to be conducted at his new laboratory in Orange
November 22 Executes patent application for new phonograph
December 19 Replies to ofﬁcial inquiry regarding the use of electricity for capital punishment
December 23 Lights Glenmont by electricity from new Orange laboratory.
