1876

 April Sets up new laboratory and household at Menlo Park, N.J.
April 3 Executes first patent applications (on acoustic telegraphy) since moving to Menlo Park (his second and third applications after a yearlong hiatus).
April 5 Sells his stock in Domestic Telegraph Co
April 11 Atlantic and Pacific Telegraph Co. files suit in New York state court against Edison, Lemuel Serrell, Western Union, and George Prescott over rights to Edison's quadruplex telegraph patents
May 1
  • Signs agreement with Marshall Lefferts regarding foreign rights to the electric pen
  • Begins experimenting in new laboratory, working on a system of acoustic transfer multiple telegraphy.
May 10 Centennial Exhibition, at which Edison's inventions are displayed, opens in Philadelphia
May 11 With George Harrington and Josiah Reiff files suit against Western Union, George Prescott, Interior Secretary Zacha­ riah Chandler, and Patent Commissioner Rudolphus Duell in Washington, D.C., to prevent any of Edison's quadruplex patents from issuing jointly with Prescott and being assigned to Western Union.
May 17 With George Harrington and Josiah Reiff sues Atlantic and Pacific and Jay Gould in federal court in New York to force payment of money owed to old Automatic Telegraph Co. investors for Edison's automatic telegraph inventions.
May 26 Renews five year agreement with Gold and Stock Telegraph Co.regarding his patent rights and salary
May 31 Executes an extensive caveat covering his acoustic transfer telegraph system
June 7 Learns of impending patent interferences with Elisha Gray and Alexander Graham Bell over acoustic telegraph inventions
June 25 Alexander Graham Bell demonstrates telephone at Centennial Exhibition
June Given awards at Centennial Exhibition for his automatic telegraph system and his electric pen and autographic press
July 3 Marshall Lefferts, Edison's mentor and president of Gold and Stock, dies
July 10 Sells British rights to the electric pen to John Breckon and Thomas Clare
July 18 Visited at Menlo Park by British scientist/engineer William Thomson
July 24 Conducts etheric force experiments in response to published criticisms by Elihu Thomson and Edwin Houston
July 25 Begins tests of acoustic transfer telegraph over line to Philadelphia.
July Conducts first sustained series of telephone experiments
August 3 Begins two months of experiments with his electromoto­ graph, including its use as an automatic telegraph repeater and as a galvanometer
August 15 John Breckon and Thomas Clare establish the Electric Writing Co. to market the electric pen in Great Britain
August 28 Receives offer for his Port Huron and Gratiot Street Railway Co. stock
August Files statements in patent interference conflicts with Elisha Gray over acoustic multiple telegraph designs.
September 7 Conceives Morse telegraph recorder Irepeater.
September 13 Begins experimenting with octruplex acoustic transfer over line to Philadelphia.
Summer Experiments with carbonized paper in an effort to make elec­ trical resistances, and also considers using carbonized paper for battery carbons, chemical crucibles, and other purposes.
October 18? Designs and soon experiments with sewing machine motor that consists of tuning forks set in motion by electromagnets
October 30 Executes two patent applications, one for a tuning fork motor and the other for a telegraph recorder/repeater that uses a punching apparatus to record messages
November 3 Discovers that the laboratory's chemical stocks have been largely damaged by sunlight and begins extensive series of observations of and experiments with the chemicals, publishing some of his observations in the American Chemist
November 28
  • Agrees to have Western Electric Manufacturing Co. become manufacturer and domestic sales agent for the electric pen and press copying system.
  • With Edward Johnson incorporates the American Novelty Co. to sell miscellaneous inventions.
December 1 Invents "duplicating ink', for making multiple copies of documents, which is subsequently marketed by American Novlty.
December 10 Laboratory building damaged by storm winds, and chemical stocks further damaged by subzero temperatures

1877

 January 6 Resolves two patent interference cases over acoustic multiple telegraphy designs with Elisha Gray by making and receiving formal concessions of priority on various points
January 8 Sketches several ideas for the application of small electric motors that could be marketed by American Novelty
Mid-January Begins extensive series of chemical and etheric force experiments
January 17
  • Begins two months of development work on a rotary, high speed press for electric pen stencils, much of which is done by Charles Batchelor, who begins to resume role as Edison's chief experimenter
  • With Charles Batchelor proposes a plan to George Bliss for establishing a foreign electric pen company
January 18 Suit against Edison by former Newark landlords of Ameri­ can Telegraph Works, Ezra and Roscoe Gould, commences
January 19 Agrees with Charles Batchelor and Edward Johnson to as­ sign his duplicating ink, Batchelor's door indicator, and Johnson's ribbon mucilage to American Novelty
January 20 First experiments with a telephone transmitter that varies electrical resistance of a circuit by changing the pressure on carbon rather than the amount of carbon
January 29
  • Proposes new agreement with Western Union for support of his laboratory
  • Stockholders of the two competing Port Huron street railways agree to consolidation after receiving a proposal from Edison
February 2 Complains to Jay Gould about his relations with Thomas Eckert and Atlantic and Pacific.
February 3 Executes a patent application for an embossing recorder/ repeater
February 8 Conducts first experiments with a two plate embossing recorder/repeater
February 9 Western Union electrician George Prescott visits the Menlo Park laboratory to examine the recorder/repea ter and Edison's telephones
February 13 Edward Johnson proposes name change from American Nov­ elty Co. to Electro Chemical Manufacturing Co
Mid-February Begins sustained work on telephone
February 19 Urges Jay Gould to settle dispute with Western Union over rights to his quadruplex inventions
February 26 Anson Stager and George Bliss visit Menlo Park to discuss foreign rights to electric pen
February 28
  • Leaves for Port Huron to effect the merger of the Port Huron and Gratiot Street Railway Co. with the opposition line and to discuss his interest in the Sarnia Street Railway Co. with other stockholders
  • Charles Batchelor accompanies Edison to Port Huron and then goes to Chicago to settle electric pen accounts with Western Electric, returning to Menlo Park on 8 March
March 12 Returns to Menlo Park from Port Huron
March 14 Displays two-plate embossing recorder/repeater at Western Union headquarters in New York
March 18 Devises first electromotograph telephone receiver
March 19 Demonstrates his telephone instruments to Western Union officials in New York over a line to Menlo Park
March 21 Edison's father, Samuel, arrives in Menlo Park for a visit
Winter-Spring Experiments on waterproof varnishes for paper barrels for the New York Paper Barrel Co
March 22 Signs agreement with Western Union for regular financial support of the Menlo Park laboratory in exchange for all of Edison's future telegraph and telephone patents
March 23 Begins to prepare applications for a telephone patent and for a patent on his new sextuplex (six message) telegraph system
March 30 Receives permission from William Orton to have Joseph Murray build six two-plate embossing recorder/repeaters
April 1 Devises telephone design that becomes the basis for his later claims to invention of the microphone
April 6 Laboratory staff's "pet" bear gets loose and they kill it.
April 10 Begins experiments on telephone transmitter designs relying upon variations of pressure on semiconductors (primarily carbon) and devises pressure relay
April 18 Executes the first of his 1877 telephone patent applications.
April 24 Signs agreement with Batchelor, George Bliss, and Charles Holland for marketing the electric pen in Europe
April 26 Begins testimony in New York in the "Quadru plex Case," Atlantic (5 Pacific v. Prescott (5 others)
April 28 Begins weeklong exhibition of his "musical" (electromoto­ graph) telephone at the Newark Opera House
May 3 Finishes primary testimony in quadruplex case
May 16 Signs agreement with his nephew, Charles Edison, and former electric pen agent George Caldwell to exhibit his musical telephone
May 28 Visited at Menlo Park laboratory by British telegraph engineer William Preece
May 31
  • Begins monthlong investigation of plumbago mixtures for telephone transmitter.
  • Signs an agreement with George Prescott and Gerritt Smith to pool their British quadruplex patents.
June 4 Electro Chemical Manufacturing transfers all of its duplicating ink and ribbon mucilage business to George Bliss and Charles Holland, who had acquired foreign rights a month earlier
June 5 Loses a patent interference case on acoustic multiple telegraphy to Elisha Gray
June 6 Designs combination telephone transmitter(electromotograph receiver)
June 8 Tests sextuplex system on Western Union line from New York to Boston after extensive experiments in laboratory and then abandons its development in early July.
June 28 Quadruplex Case final arguments conclude
July 2 Samuel Edison leaves Menlo Park for Port Huron
July 3 Demonstrates embossing recorder/repeater to Western Union officials and British telegraph officials William Preece and Henry Fischer
July 17 Conceives telephone message recorder/repeater
July 18 Conceives phonograph
July 19 Edward Johnson and George Barker exhibit Edison's musi­ cal telephone before large crowd at the Permanent Exhibition Hall in Philadelphia
July 19? Finishes preparing preliminary specification for British telephone patent, which includes description of phonograph
July 21 Visited at Menlo Park laboratory by scientists George Barker and Henry Draper, and electrical manufacturer William Wallace
July 27 Visited at Menlo Park laboratory by Alexander Siemens, nephew of William Siemens
July 30 Begins to develop carbon "fluff" for telephone transmitters
August 1 Edward Johnson gives second telephone exhibition concert in Philadelphia and then begins a series of concerts along the eastern seaboard that continues through the end of the year
August 3 Begins monthlong correspondence with Henry Draper regarding spectroscopy
August 4 to 10 Conducts telephone experiments with Thomas David at Menlo Park laboratory and over a line in lower Manhattan
August 7 Sends Charles Edison to Port Huron to represent his interest in street railways
August 10 Exhibits combination transmitter electromotograph receivers built by Joseph Murray to Western Union officials and begins telephone line tests in New York City
Mid-August Tests various telephone designs over lines in New York City.
August 20 Demonstrates a new telephone design to William Orton and other Western Union officials
August 21 Western Union announces agreement to purchase control of Atlantic and Pacific, thus ending commercial and legal conflicts between the corporations
August 24 Begins designing handheld telephones
August 25 Visited at Menlo Park by Robert Watson of Montreal, who wants to introduce Edison's telephone in Canada
September 9 Resigns his position as Atlantic and Pacific electrician. Conducts experiments with electric arc lights
September 10 Files depositions by himself, Batchelor, and Adams in electric pen patent interference case against Henry Trueman and gives evidence in hand-stamp patent interference with A. E. Hix
September 10 Conducts first experiments with incandescent electric lighting
September 11 to 13 With laboratory staff goes fishing in Raritan Bay
September 15 Signs an agreement with Franklin Badger, associate of Robert Watson, regarding the marketing of his telephone in Canada
September 17 Demonstrates a new transmitter to William Orton, who orders 150 sets of transmitters and receivers for Western Union subsidiary Gold and Stock
September 18
  • Proposes to George Bliss that Bliss and Charles Holland sell rights to European telephone patents
  • With George Prescott signs agreement with Stephen Field and Cornelius Herz regarding European quadruplex patents
September 28 Learns of successful quadruplex telegraph tests conducted on British Post Office lines by Gerritt Smith and George Hamilton
September 29 Learns first telephone patent application will be suspended pending resolution of patent interference conflicts
October 5 to 8 Redesigns telephone transmitter being manufactured by Joseph Murray
October 6 Wins electric pen patent interference against Henry Trueman
October 18 Participates in Edward Johnson's telephone exhibition at Jersey City
October 22 Begins using disks of a plumbago rubber mixture in place of fluff in telephone transmitter
October 26 Begins using disks of a plumbago-rubber mixture in place of fluff in telephone transmitter
November 17 Western Union establishes the American Speaking Telephone Co. to combine its interest in Edison's telephone patents with the Harmonic Telegraph Co.'s ownership of Elisha Gray's patents
Mid November Devises induction coil circuit for telephone
November Explores alternative, noncarbon transmitter designs
December 3 Tests induction-coil circuit for telephone
December 6 John Kruesi makes first tin-foil, cylinder phonograph
December 7 Demonstrates his cylinder phonograph at Scientific American office in New York.
December 12 Wins electric pen patent interference against Edward Stewar
December 15 Executes first phonograph patent application.
December 16 Receives old "Telegraphy" entry for Appleton's Cyclopedia of Applied Mechanics, which he has been commissioned to revise.
December 17 Signs agreements with George Bliss and Hungarian pro­ moter Theodore Puskas regarding the sale and exploitation of his European telephone and phonograph patents
December 27 Confers at Menlo Park with Edward Johnson, Uriah Painter, and Gardiner Hubbard about their proposals that he break his contract with Western Union and let them form a company to market his phonograph
December 29 Proposed as a scientific member of the American commission to the 1878 Paris Universal Exposition
December 31 Demonstrates phonograph to William Orton
