2/11/1847 Is born in Milan, Ohio.
1847-54 Lives in Milan
1854-63 Lives in Port Huron, Mich.
1859-60 Winter Starts selling newspapers and candy on the trains of the Grand Trunk Railroad.
Spring of 1862
Publishes and prints on the train his own newspaper, the Weekly Herald.
Fall 1862 Studies telegraphy with James Mackenzie, station agent at Mount Clemens, Mich.
Winter 1862-63 Begins work as a telegraph operator in Micah Walker's book and jewelry store in Port Huron.
Late Spring-Summer 1863 Starts job as a telegrapher for the Grand Trunk Railroad at Stratford Junction, Ont.
1863-1864
 Returns briefly to Port Huron.
Works the night shift as a railroad telegrapher near Adrian, Mich., where he meets Ezra Gilliland for the first time.
1864-65 Fall-Winter
 Is employed for two months as a railroad telegrapher in Fort Wayne, Ind.
1864-65 Fall-Winter Works in the Indianapolis, Ind., office of the Western Union Telegraph Co. and experiments on improvements in tele­ graph repeaters.
1865 Spring-Fall
Works in the Cincinnati, Ohio, office of Western Union and experiments on self.:.adjusting relays.
17 September September
 Becomes a founding member of the Cincinnati District of the National Telegraphic Union.
  Is promoted to telegraph operator first class.
  Begins designing devices for multiple telegraphy.
1865-66 Fall-Spring Becomes the regular press-wire operator in the Memphis, Tenn., office of the South-Western Telegraph Co.conducts repeater experiments
Spring 1866 Enters Western Union's Louisville, Ky., office as a press-wire operator.
6/4/1866 Transfers his membership in the National Telegraphic Union to the Louisville District.
8/1/1866 Leaves for New Orleans, La., planning to embark for Brazil. Returns to the Western Union office in Louisville after a short stay in Port Huron.
Summer 1867 Returns to the Western Union office in Cincinnati.
10/1/1867 Returns to Port Huron.
March-April 1868 Begins_work as an operator at the main Western Union office in Boston, Mass
4/11/1868 Publishes in the Telegrapher the first of several articles on his telegraph inventions and on the Boston telegraph community.
7/1/1868 Makes the first of several agreements with E. Baker Welch, a Boston businessman who helps finance his early inventive work.
7/28/1868 Signs a caveat for a fire alarm telegraph and assigns the in­vention to Welch.
10/13/1868 Signs a patent application for an electric vote recorder, which later issues as his first patent.
1/21/1869 Sells rights in his first successful printing telegraph, the Bos­ ton instrument, to Boston businessmenjoel Hills and Wil­liam Plummer.
1/30/1869 Announces his resignation from his job with Western Union in order to devote himself full time to inventing and to pur­suing various telegraph enterprises.
Winter-Spring 1869 Joins Frank Hanaford in establishing a business to produce and sell private-line telegraphs at 9 Wilson Lane in Boston.
4/13/1869 Tries and fails to make his new double transmitter work be­ tween Rochester and New York City.
April-May 1869 Moves to New York City.
6/21/1869 Is issued his first telegraph patent (for the Boston instru­ment).
8/1/1869 Replaces Franklin Pope as superintendent of Samuel Laws's Gold and Stock Reporting Telegraph Co. in New York City and makes improvements on Laws's stock printer.
9/12/1869 Moves to Elizabeth, N.L and boards with Pope's mother.
10/2/1869 Joins his partners Pope and James Ashley in advertising their newly formed Pope, Edison & Co. as a firm of electrical en­gineers and telegraph contractors.
Fall 1870 Operates a small shop in the electrical instrument factory of Leverett Bradley in Jersey City, NJ.
2/10/1870 Signs two contracts with George Field and Elisha Andrews of the Gold and Stock Telegraph Co. that provide funds for developing inventions and establishing a shop.
2/5/1870 Joins William Unger in establishing his first major shop, the Newark Telegraph Works.
4/18/1870 Joins Pope and Ashley in assigning to the Gold and Stock Telegraph Co. their rights to printing telegraph patents. Engages Lemuel Serrell as patent attorney.
5/1/1870 Engages Lemuel Serrell as patent attorney.
7/1/1870 Joins Pope, Ashley, Marshall Clifford Lefferts, and William Allen in establishing the American Printing Telegraph Co., an enterprise for providing private-line telegraphs.
8/3/1870 Signs an agreement with Daniel Craig to invent an improved perforator for automatic telegraphy.
10/1/1870 Signs an agreement with George Harrington making them partners in the American Telegraph Works and providing Edison with funds for automatic telegraph experiments.
10/19/1870 Negotiates with Marshall Lefferts to sell his newly designed universal private-line printer to Gold and Stock
10/26/1870 Charles Batchelor begins employment at the American Tele­ graph Works.
11/28/1870 The Automatic Telegraph Co. is incorporated and Harring­ ton is named president.
12/1/1870 Pope, Edison & Co. announces its dissolution
Winter-Spring 1871 Designs perforators, transmitters, ink recorders, and type­ writers for automatic telegraphy.
4/4/1871 Gives Harrington power of attorney for disposition of Edi­son's share in all inventions relating to automatic telegraphy.
4/9/1871 Edison's mother, Nancy, dies in Port Huron.
April-May 1871
 Moves the Newark Telegraph Works from Railroad Ave. to Ward St. and changes the company's name to Edison and Unger.
Manufactures his cotton instrument, developed for Gold and Stock under his contract with Field and Andrews.
5/26/1871 Sells the rights to his existing and future printing telegraph patents to Gold and Stock and becomes the company's con­ sulting electrician.
28-29July Begins series of four notebooks to record his inventive work on printing, automatic, and other forms of telegraphy.
August Begins manufacturing his universal stock printer for Gold and Stock.
October Employs Mary Stilwell for his News Reporting Telegraph Co., which sought to provide general and conunercial news in Newark.
11/22/1871 Purchases his first house, located on Wright St. in Newark.
11/28/1871 Buys stock in the street railway of his brother, William Pitt Edison, in Port Huron.
12/25/1871 Marries Mary Stilwell.
15-17 January 1872 Designs a district telegraph that he assigns to the American District Telegraph Co.
1/27/1872 Transforms his universal private-line printer into an electric typewriter for automatic telegraphy.
January-February 1872 Fills laboratory notebooks with variations for his universal stock printer and his universal private-line printer.
2/5/1872 Becomes a parmer in J. T. Murray and Co., which later be­ comes Edison and Murray.
5/1/1872 Delivers first models of improved universal private-line printer to Gold and Stock.
May-June 1872 Supplies his universal stock printer to the Exchange Tele­ graph Co. of London
7/3/1872 Agrees to purchase Unger's share in Edison and Unger, thereby dissolving their parmership.
11/5/1873 Makes an agreement with Josiah Reiff to provide Edison with an annual salary while he works on automatic telegraph im­ provements.
12/14/1872 The Automatic Telegraph Co. opens for business using Edi­ son's automatic telegraph improvements.
Fall Conducts tests of his automatic telegraph system on the lines of the Automatic Telelgraph Co. and the Southern and At­ lantic Telegraph Co.
2/10/1873 Meets with William Orton, president of Western Union, and makes a verbal agreement to develop duplex telegraphy.
2/18/1873 Edison's first daughter, Marion Estelle ("Dot"), is born in Newark.
3/31/1873 Agrees to develop a roman letter automatic telegraph for Harrington and Reiff
9-22 April 1873 Prepares ten patent applications on duplex telegraphy
4/23/1873 Leaves for England
23-27 May 1873 Conducts tests of his automatic telegraph system for the British Post Office
1-15June 1873 Conducts tests of his automatic telegraph on a cable stored at the Greenwich works of the Telegraph Construction and Maintenance Co
6/25/1873 Arrives back in the United States
