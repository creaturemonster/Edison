Date

Event

1878

 January 1 to 3 Exhibits phonograph at Western Union offices in New York
January 3 Designs flywheel phonograph
January 7 Contracts for the development and manufacture of phonographic toys and clocks
January 8 to 10 Tests telephone at Western Union's New York offices, further straining relations with company electricians
January 12 With George Prescott and Gerritt Smith, makes William Orton trustee of British quadruplex patent rights
January 23 Theodore Puskas leaves for Europe as Edison's agent for the phonograph and telephone
January 29 to 30 With Charles Batchelor, visits Ansonia (Conn.) Clock Co. and experiments with applying the phonograph to clocks
January 30 Signs agreement for commercial exploitation of the phonograph in the United States
January
  • Makes flywheel phonographs to give to prominent scientific and technological figures
  • British investors in Edison's automatic telegraph assert claim to the quadruplex, beginning prolonged legal contest
January-February Edward Johnson undertakes lecture tours promoting Edison's phonograph and carbon telephone.
February 1 William Preece and John Tyndall demonstrate the phono­ graph at the Royal Institution in London, the first exhibition outside the United States
February 4 With Henry Bentley, begins testing the carbon telephone transmitter between New York and Philadelphia
February 6 Completes design of small demonstration phonograph to be sold at the Paris Universal Exposition
February 19 Issued first phonograph patent.
February 22 Plans series of experiments to develop a telephone receiver that does not infringe Bell patents
February 28 Executes extensive phonograph and telephone caveats and executes three patent applications, for telephone stations and call signal apparatus, for preventing interference between telephone lines, and for the aerophone
March 1 Sends James Adams to Philadelphia to assist Henry Bentley with telephone tests
March 2 to 8 Edward Johnson becomes general agent of prospective pho­ nograph company and orders manufacture of first commercial phonographs
March 11 Theodore Puskas demonstrates the phonograph at the Academy of Sciences in Paris, the first public demonstration in Continental Europe
March 12 Devises improved carbon telephone transmitter
March 15 Begins negotiations with Western Union presider:it William Orton for a new telephone contract with tthe company
March 19 Sends James Adams to London with telephone transmitters and receivers to be tested on British Post Office lines
March 20 First public demonstration of improved carbon telephone transmitter at the Franklin Institute in Philadelphia
Winter-Spring Experiments with hand-powered phonographs and attempts to develop clockwork driven cylinder and disk phonographs. Expands Menlo Park laboratory staff.
March 22 Signs agreement, negotiated by Theodore Puskas, with the London Stereoscopic and Photographic Co. for commercial exploitation of the phonograph in Great Britain
March 26 Notification of patent interferences marks beginning of long contest over telephone patents
March 29 The New York World publishes first description of Edison's medicinal preparation for relieving pain ("polyform").
March Newspapers begin extensive coverage of Edison, which helps to spread his fame and produces crowds of visitors to Menlo Park
March-April Discusses with Gardiner Hubbard the possibility of Bell Telephone Co. acquiring rights to the carbon transmitter.
April 1 The New York Daily Graphic publishes April Fool's hoax describing Edison's "Food Creator."
April 4 Returns signed membership forms to the Theosophical Society
April 6 Makes first of several loans to Joseph Murray to pay back rent on Ward Street shop in Newark
April 8 Biographical sketch by George Bliss published in the Chicago Tribune
April 10 Dubbed "Wizard of Menlo Park" by the New York Daily Graphic
April 18 to 19 Demonstrates phonograph for the National Academy of Sciences, members of Congress, and President Rutherford B. Hayes
April 19 The Wa_shington Star publishes first account of Edison's tasimeter principle and describes his hearing aid device, prompting numerous inquiries
April 19 William Orton dies
April 24 Edison Speaking Phonograph Co. is incorporated
April Publishes "The Future of the Phonograph;' ghost-written by Edward Johnson, in the North American Review.
May 1 Universal Exposition, with Edison exhibit, opens in Paris
May 6 Observes transit of Mercury with borrowed telescope
May 16 Develops new tasimeter design
May 22 Visited by a delegation of Boston newspaper reporters and demonstrates telephones, the tasimeter, phonograph, and other acoustic devices
May 29 Executes second phonograph caveat, based on European patent specifications completed in early May
May 30 Demonstrates phonograph with Charles Batchelor and several other associates at the Convent of Mount St. Vincent, a Catholic girls' school near Yonkers, N.Y
May 31 Executes agreement assigning telephone patents to Western Union for six thousand dollars annually for seventeen years
May Edison Speaking Phonograph Co. hires James Redpath to manage phonograph exhibitions and begins training exhibitors
May to June Develops new hand crank phonograph for exhibition. Arranges phonograph and telephone sales agencies for Australia and Central and South America
June 1 Makes George Gouraud his agent for telephone in Great Britain
June 2 Starts nephew Charles Edison on experiments to develop a noninfringing telephone receiver, which continue through the end of he year
June 8 Publishes letter in the New York Daily Tribune asserting priority to microphone principle claimed by David Hughes, beginning protracted dispute
June 17 Orders 25 transmitting and receiving telephone sets for sale abroad from Partrick and Carter in Philadelphia
June 26 Receives first honorary doctorate, from Union College in Schenectady, N.Y
Spring Attempts to develop hearins aid and other acoustic devices
July 3 With Charles Batchelor, begins to investigate causes of noise along New York's Metropolitan Elevated Railroad
July 8 Writes letter to the Chemical News asserting priority over Edwin Houston and Elihu Thomson for the telephone repeater
July 13 Leaves from New York with George Barker for solar eclipse expedition to Rawlins, Wyo., and a month long vacation in the western United States
July 17 Begins funding Patrick Kenny's facsimile telegraph experiments
July 20 Purchases hunting and fishing equipment in Laramie, Wyo
July 29 Attempts to measure heat of solar corona with tasimeter during total eclipse
July to August Charles Batchelor and Edward Johnson make improvements to exhibition phonograph.
August 1 to 3 Visits San Francisco
August 6 to 7 Tours Yosemite and stays overnight in nearby Mariposa,Calif.
August 9 Spends night at Virginia City, Nev., where he inspects mines and discusses problems of heat and ventilation in the shafts
August 15 Goes hunting and fishing in Wyoming
August 21 Visits George Bliss in Chicago
August 22 Receives news of award of a Grand Prize from the Paris Universal Exposition
August 23 Presented as new member of the American Association for the Adv'ancement of Science at the annual meeting in St. Louis, and delivers a paper on the tasimeter.
August 24 Returns to Chicago
August 26 Arrives in Menlo Park
August 27 Begins electric light experiments.
September 1 Edward Johnson begins exhibition of improved phonograph in New York City and later includes other Edison inventions
September 8 With Charles Batchelor, George Barker and Charles Chandler, visits William Wallace's shop in Ansonia, Conn., to see electric light and power apparatus
September 10 Drafts first electric lighting caveat
September 13 to 16 Announces he has solved problem of incandescent electric lighting.
September 14 Receives dynamo from William Wallace
September 19 Begins to receive offers from prospective electric lighting investors
September 21 Theodore Puskas forms company for commercial exploitation of Edison and Elisha Gray telephones in France
September 27 Drafts second electric lighting caveat
October 3 Agrees to allow Grosvenor Lowrey to conduct negotjations with prospective investors
October 5 Executes first patent application for electric lighting.
October 5 to 18 Works on new phonograph for business dictation
October 16 Edison Electric Light Co. incorporated, principally by investors connected with Western Union and Drexel, Morgan & Co
October 23-26 Confined to bed by facial neuralgia
October 26 Edison's second son, William Leslie, is born in Menlo Park
October 30 Angered by investors' concern over William Sawyer's and Albon Man's joint claim to priority in electric lighting
October
  • Executes four patent caveats on electric lighting.
  • Purchases a new steam engine, a Weston dynamo, and a second Wallace dynamo.
  • Begins construction of new mjichine shop and office buildings
November 6 Conducts telegraphic conversation with Lemuel Serrell about filing new lamp patent in Britain
November 13 Hires Francis Upton to conduct search of relevant technical and scientific literature in order to allay fears of Edison Electric Light Co. investors
November 15 Agrees to assign lighting patents to Edison Electric Light Company for thirty thousand dollars plus stock and royalties
November 20 Designs two dynamos, one of which is included in a British patent application
November 22 Begins negotiating with Gold and Stock regarding new electromotograph telephone receiver
November 23 With Edison's support, Uriah Painter and Edward Johnson take control of Edison Speaking Phonograph Co
November 29-30 Designs first electric meter
November-December
  • Displays various electric lighting devices at Menlo Park for Edison Electric Light Co. investors.
  • Compares operating costs of gas, arc, and incandescent light­ ing systems.
  • Negotiates through Grosvenor Lowrey with Drexel, Morgan & Co. for foreign rights to electric lighting inventions
December 3 Executes first patent application for an electric generator, based on earlier tuning fork engine
December 14 Laboratory workers begin moving equipment into new building
December 15 Hires Francis Upton as mathematical and experimental assistant
December Acquires Siemens and Gramme dynamos and begins extensive tests of existing generator technology
Late December Designs and begins to build new dynamo
